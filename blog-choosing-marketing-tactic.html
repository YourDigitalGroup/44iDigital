Choosing the Right Marketing Tactic

Samantha Escalante
Blog4 minMarch 23, 2026

Photo by Dylan Gillis on Unsplash

With so many ways to help clients promote their businesses, identifying the most effective marketing tactics can feel overwhelming. The reality is that marketing is not a one-size-fits-all solution. What drives success for one business may not deliver the same results for another. That's why a strategic approach is essential. In this article, we'll explore how to evaluate different tactics and determine which ones will best position your client for success.

Determining Goals and Needs

The foundation of any successful marketing strategy starts with a clear understanding of your client's goals and needs. Do they need to sell more of their products or services? Do they need to build brand awareness? Or are they a new business starting fresh? Defining these priorities early ensures recommendations are aligned not just with their desired outcomes, but with what they need to be successful long-term.

Remember, it is equally important to assess the client's current standing. A business without a website or active social media presence will benefit far more from building a strong foundation than jumping straight into advanced tactics like paid search. Taking the time to build a strategy that both meets goals and is realistic with their current presence not only sets a clear direction but also helps establish realistic expectations and long-term success.

Avoid Buzzword Tactics

Clients often are influenced by trending or buzzworthy tactics. While these options may sound appealing, they aren't always the right fit. It's important to evaluate whether a tactic truly supports the client's goals and needs rather than simply following what's popular.

For example, advertising on streaming platforms like YouTube TV may seem like an exciting option, but it typically requires a larger market size and longer-term investment to be effective. For clients in smaller markets or those seeking short-term results, alternative solutions, such as standard streaming TV or traditional YouTube advertising, may be a better fit to achieve the client's goals and expectations.

By assisting clients in finding the right tactic for them, you can avoid unmet goals or expectations down the road.

Ultimately, choosing the right marketing tactic comes down to strategy, not trends. By taking the time to clearly understand your client's goals and evaluate which channels truly align with their needs, you can build a plan that is both realistic and results-driven.
