Boosted Posts vs. Social Ads

Kennedy Pirlet
Blog4 minAugust 26, 2024

Incorporating social media into your marketing strategy is an excellent way to connect with your audience on a personal level. Leveraging social media ads can significantly enhance your business’s reach and social presence. Facebook, one of the most popular social networking platforms, offers various paid advertising options. Let us help you discover the benefits and drawbacks of the different ad formats available on Facebook.

The Basics of Boosted Posts

Boosting posts on your business page’s timeline helps connect your brand with your customers and is great for audience engagement, brand awareness, and visibility. These posts create a sense of a virtual word of mouth, which can extend your reach as people interact with and share your content.

The process of boosting is simple: you choose a post that has already been published on your page at any point, set a budget, select your audience, and determine the duration of the boost. This easy process makes it accessible even for those new to Facebook marketing.

However, boosted posts have limited audience targeting and placement options, meaning you have less control over who sees your posts in comparison to Facebook Ads. This can be a disadvantage if your campaign goal is to reach a specific demographic audience.

The Basics of Facebook Ads

Facebook Ads are paid advertisements created through Facebook Ad Manager to promote products or services to specific audiences. These ads use Facebook’s algorithm and more advanced filtering to target your wanted demographic, are more customizable and can use various media forms including static images, videos, infographics and carousel ads.

The Ad Manager allows you to set specific campaign objectives like driving clicks, impressions, and website traffic. You can also track detailed analytics to see how your ads are performing, allowing you to adjust your campaign accordingly. Facebook Ads are designed to run over a longer period of time, and usually need a larger budget to obtain the best results.

While Facebook Ads Manager provides advanced targeting options, it can often be less user-friendly for those navigating its features.

When to Use Each Option

Consider using Facebook Ads when you need advanced targeting to reach specific audience segments or require detailed analytics to measure and adjust your campaign. This is particularly useful for campaigns aimed at driving conversions like online sales and event registrations where precise targeting is essential. Facebook Ads are also the ideal choice for long-term campaigns that require ongoing adjustments based on your performance data.

If you want to increase visibility and engagement for a specific post, try boosted posts. This approach is beneficial for more short-term goals like promoting a limited-time offer or creating buzz around a new product launch. Boosting posts is also a good option when you have a limited budget and would prefer a straightforward approach.

Overall, Facebook Ads provide advanced features and targeting for comprehensive marketing campaigns, while Boosted posts offer a quick and easy way to amplify individual posts. By understanding their differences and strengths, you can effectively integrate both into your marketing strategy to achieve your business goals.
